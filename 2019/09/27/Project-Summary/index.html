Project Summary

2019-09-27

What is my project?

My project studies the dehydration process occurred when generating isobutylene from tert-butyl alcohol (TBA). Dehydration of TBA contributes to the conversion rate of isobutylene. I focus on a scale-up study based on a lab-scale experiment using a mathematical model.

What are my research target and research method?

There are two main targets in this project. On one hand, I investigate the dehydration process in isobutylene production which potentially can increase the conversion rate in industrial production. I looked for papers that studied in depth this particular reaction and how the product of this reaction could benefit the modern industrial world. Also, a numerical model is built to tackle this problem. On the other hand, as I am interested in chemistry and would like to do an extracurriculum project to further my study in chemistry.

What was the biggest challenge that I encountered? How did I overcome it?

The biggest challenge was learning and understanding MatLab and how to calculate the results. I used sources such as stackoverflow and github to help myself understand the software more and aid myself in the complex calculations.

What are my research result(s) and conclusion(s)?

For this reaction, the concentration of water decreases the reaction rate because as the reaction progresses, more water is present in the system because water is a product of this reaction. And as more water is present in the system, the reaction rate slows down. Thus in order for this reaction to be successfully implemented in an industrial fashion, the dehydration of the reactants is crucial.
